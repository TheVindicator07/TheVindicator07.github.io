Attractions

Milford Sound

Location:

South West New Zealand (Fiordland National Park)

About:

Named Piopiotahi in Maori, and named after Milford Haven in Wales, Milford Sound is perhaps New Zealands most famous tourist attraction, attracting anywhere between 550,000 and 1,000,000 tourists every year. Typical activities performed here include: Tramping, Canoeing and 1-2 hour long boat rides. It is also a common scenic route for Cruise Ships.

Tongariro National Park

Location:

Central North Island

About:

Tongariro is New Zealands oldest National Park and the fourth established National Park in the world. It is home to the Tongariro Alpine Crossing, which is a popular Hiking trail and is regarded as one of the best in the world. During the Winter however, Skiing and Snowboarding are the preferred activities, performed on the Whakapapa Ski fields, on the north west side of Mt Ruapehu.

Hobbiton

Location:

Matamata

About:

The Hobbiton film set is a popular tourist location 10km south of Matamata, and was a major filming location for the Lord Of The Rings and The Hobbit film trilogies which now offers 2-3 hour long guided tours. In 2013, the set surpassed 500,000 visitors and as of 2017, shows no signs of slowing down.

Piha Beach

Location:

West Auckland (39km from CBD)

About:

While the Piha area is only home to around 600 residents, it is one of the most popular Summer vacation spots in the country. It is home to the Famous, Lion Rock. It is also notorious for being deceptively calm, and claiming numurous lives due to the unpredictable Riptides.

Waitomo Gloworm Caves

Location:

Waitomo

About:

Waitomo (Maori for Water "Wai" Hole "Tomo") is home to the one of a kind Glowworm caves. The Glowworms in particular are the Arachnocampa luminosa breed, which can only be found in New Zealand. The caves not only provide a guided tour, but also Black Water Rafting for those feeling adventurous.

Sky Tower

Location:

Central Auckland

About:

Completed in 1997, the Sky Tower became the Tallest building in the Southern Hemisphere, and is currently the 23rd tallest building in the world. It is home to New Zealands only revolving restaurant, and two adventurous activities: The Sky Walk, and The Sky Jump. It also has a few glass floor panels you can walk over, for those not feeling so adventurous.
